Veritome structures EU AI Act compliance as a sequential, evidence-backed journey of six phases. Each phase has a defined trigger (what must be true before you start it), a set of obligations (what you must do and document), and a set of outputs (the artefacts that prove you did it). Phases unlock sequentially: you cannot formally enter Phase 3 until Phase 2 obligations are complete, which prevents gaps in your compliance record and mirrors how the EU AI Act's own logic is organised — classification before scoping, scoping before implementation.

## The six phases

<Steps>
  <Step title="Phase 1 — Classify">
    **Trigger:** A new AI system is registered in Veritome.

    **What you do:** Run the classification wizard. The wizard screens the system against the Art. 5 prohibited-practice list first, then walks you through a structured decision tree to determine:
    - Your organisation's **role** (Provider, Deployer, Importer, Distributor — or GPAI Model Provider if you develop a foundation model).
    - The system's **risk tier** (Prohibited, High-Risk, Limited-Risk, Minimal-Risk).
    - The applicable **Annex III domain** (Biometrics, Critical Infrastructure, Education, Employment, Essential Services, Law Enforcement, Migration, Justice & Democracy), if any.
    - Whether an **Art. 6(3) not-high-risk exception** applies to an Annex III system.

    **What Veritome produces:** A timestamped **classification record** stored against the system. Every re-classification also creates a new record, preserving the full audit trail. The classification result drives every subsequent phase — role and risk tier are the two primary inputs to the Obligation Engine.

    **Completion signal:** Classification confirmed by an authorised user. The CLASSIFY phase obligations appear as immediately complete once you confirm.

    <Note>
      If you discover that a system should be re-classified (e.g. its intended purpose changes, or new guidance from the AI Office redefines a domain), run the wizard again. Re-classification requires a justification note and triggers an obligation regeneration. Users with the **Re-classify systems** permission can do this; by default, Compliance managers and Org admins hold this permission.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Phase 2 — Scope & Literacy">
    **Trigger:** Phase 1 (Classify) complete.

    **What you do:** Review the obligation list generated by the Obligation Engine — the complete set of EU AI Act duties applicable to this system — and confirm the scope. You also address any **Art. 4 AI literacy** obligations, which are organisation-level (one instance across your whole organisation, not per system) and appear in this phase. AI literacy obligations cover ensuring that staff involved in operating, overseeing, or deploying AI systems have an appropriate level of understanding of AI technology, its capabilities, limitations, and risks.

    **What Veritome produces:** A confirmed obligation set — the active list of System and Organisation obligations for this system. The phase bar chart on the dashboard begins populating. Aria can suggest which obligations to prioritise based on your risk tier and domain.

    **Completion signal:** All SCOPE phase obligations reach **Complete** or **Not Applicable** status.

    <Tip>
      Use this phase to assign obligation owners. Each obligation or tab can be assigned to a specific team member. Assigning work early prevents obligations from sitting in "Not Started" until they become overdue.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Phase 3 — Build & Document">
    **Trigger:** Phase 2 (Scope & Literacy) complete.

    **What you do:** This is the largest phase — the core implementation work. For High-Risk systems (and Providers of any risk tier), you build and document the compliance infrastructure the EU AI Act requires. Key obligations in this phase include:

    | Article | Obligation |
    |---------|-----------|
    | Art. 9 | Risk management system — document and test risks throughout the AI lifecycle |
    | Art. 10 | Data governance — training, validation, and testing data requirements |
    | Art. 11 | Technical documentation (Annex IV) — the full technical dossier for the system |
    | Art. 12 | Record-keeping — automatic logging of system operation |
    | Art. 13 | Transparency to deployers — instructions for use (IFU package) |
    | Art. 14 | Human oversight — design measures and operational procedures |
    | Art. 17 | Quality management system (Providers only) |
    | Art. 26 | Deployer-specific obligations — monitoring, human oversight, incident reporting |

    **What Veritome produces:** Completed smart forms for each obligation, each backed by evidence attachments and checklist completions. Taken together, these forms constitute the **technical documentation dossier** that Providers must maintain under Art. 11. On the Core plan (€149/month) and above, Veritome can generate a consolidated PDF dossier for regulator submission.

    **Completion signal:** All IMPLEMENT phase obligations reach **Complete**, **Approved**, or **Not Applicable** status.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Phase 4 — Conformity Assessment">
    **Trigger:** Phase 3 (Build & Document) complete.

    **What you do:** The assessment route depends on your role.

    **Providers** perform a conformity assessment under Art. 43:
    - For most Annex III systems, Providers conduct an **internal conformity assessment** (self-assessment against the requirements of Chapter III Section 2).
    - For certain high-risk systems (biometric identification, critical infrastructure), a **third-party notified body assessment** is required.
    - After assessment, the Provider draws up the **EU Declaration of Conformity** (Art. 47) and affixes the **CE mark** (Art. 48).

    **Deployers** do not perform Art. 43 conformity assessment — instead, they conduct a **Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (FRIA)** under Art. 27 before deployment if they are a public body or provide public-interest services (banking, insurance, essential public services). For Deployers, this phase is labelled *Fundamental-rights & pre-deployment assessment* in Veritome.

    **What Veritome produces:** Completed Art. 43 conformity assessment record, or a completed FRIA form. Providers also generate the EU Declaration of Conformity PDF, which can be downloaded from **Systems → [System] → Declaration**.

    **Completion signal:** Conformity assessment obligation marked **Complete** and approved by an authorised user.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Phase 5 — Register">
    **Trigger:** Phase 4 (Conformity Assessment) complete.

    **What you do:** Register the system in the **EU AI database** before you place it on the market or put it into service. The EU AI database is maintained by the European Commission; Veritome provides the structured data export and a guided form to ensure you capture all required fields (Art. 49, Annex VIII).

    **What Veritome produces:** A completed Annex VIII registration data record, downloadable as a structured export for submission to the EU AI database. After you complete registration externally, record the EU registration number back in Veritome — the system's card shows a **EU DB registered** badge once you do.

    **Completion signal:** Registration obligation marked **Complete** and the EU database registration number recorded.

    <Info>
      The EU AI database is phased. High-Risk systems under Annex III must be registered before placing the system on the market. GPAI model providers register models in the database from August 2025.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Phase 6 — Operate & Monitor">
    **Trigger:** Phase 5 (Register) complete.

    **What you do:** This is the ongoing operational phase — compliance does not end at deployment. Key obligations include:

    | Article | Obligation |
    |---------|-----------|
    | Art. 72 | Post-market monitoring — establish and operate a post-market monitoring system; collect and analyse performance data |
    | Art. 73 | Serious incident reporting — report serious incidents to national market surveillance authorities within defined timeframes |
    | Art. 74 | Market surveillance — cooperate with national authorities on inspections and information requests |
    | Art. 18/19 | Technical documentation retention — retain docs for 10 years after the last system is placed on the market |

    **What Veritome produces:** Incident records (logged from **Incidents** in the sidebar), post-market monitoring evidence, and retention-period tracking. Veritome alerts you when evidence items are approaching their expiry date.

    **Completion signal:** Phase 6 obligations are continuous — they recur over the system's operational lifetime. Veritome marks individual obligation instances as Complete when you submit evidence for a given review period, then resets them for the next cycle.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How phases gate each other

Phases unlock sequentially. The **phase bar chart** on the dashboard shows the current status — **Locked**, **In Progress**, or **Complete** — for each phase of each system. A phase is locked until all obligations in the preceding phase reach a terminal status (Complete, Approved, or Not Applicable).

<Warning>
  Marking an obligation **Not Applicable** requires a justification note. Veritome records the reason and the user who set the status in the audit trail. Reviewers and auditors can filter the obligation list to see all Not Applicable items and their justifications.
</Warning>

You can work on obligations within the current unlocked phase in any order — there is no enforced sequence within a phase. However, some obligations have logical dependencies (e.g. the Art. 9 risk management plan must exist before you can complete the Art. 9 risk testing checklist), and Veritome's guidance text within each obligation explains those dependencies.

## The Kanban and task model within each phase

Within each phase, obligations are surfaced in two ways:

1. **Tabs in the system view** — obligations are grouped by topic area (Risk Management, Data Governance, Technical Documentation, etc.). Each tab shows a completion bar and a list of obligations. Click an obligation to open its smart form.

2. **All Obligations tracker** (sidebar → Obligations) — a flat, filterable list across all systems. Use this for portfolio-level work, cross-system reporting, or when an owner wants to see only their assigned obligations.

Every obligation moves through the following statuses:

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Not Started** | Generated by the engine; no work begun |
| **In Progress** | A user has opened the smart form and saved partial progress |
| **Awaiting Approval** | The responsible user has submitted the obligation for four-eyes sign-off |
| **Approved** | A second authorised user has reviewed and approved the work |
| **Complete** | Obligation fully satisfied and, where required, approved |
| **Overdue** | Due date has passed and the obligation is not yet Complete |
| **Not Applicable** | Justified exemption recorded by an authorised user |

The status of each obligation feeds the compliance score calculation: Approved and Complete obligations count as 100 %; In Progress counts as partial (weighted by checklist completion); Not Started and Overdue obligations count as 0 %.
