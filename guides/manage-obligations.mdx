After the Obligation Engine maps your duties, every applicable EU AI Act obligation appears in the Obligations workbench — a Kanban board where you can track progress, assign owners, attach evidence, and route items through a four-eyes approval workflow. Obligations are tied to specific articles, have derived deadlines, and feed directly into your per-system compliance score and the organisation's overall posture on the dashboard.

## The Kanban board

Navigate to **Obligations** from the main menu to see the board. Columns represent obligation statuses and move left to right as work progresses:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Not Started" icon="circle">
    The obligation has been generated by the engine but no work has begun. All newly classified systems start here.
  </Card>
  <Card title="In Progress" icon="spinner">
    Someone has opened the obligation and started filling the smart form. The assignee is shown on the card.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Under Review" icon="eye">
    The form has been submitted and is awaiting peer approval (four-eyes). The card shows the reviewer's name and the date submitted.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Approved" icon="circle-check">
    A second team member with the required role has approved the submission. The obligation counts toward the system's compliance score.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

You can filter the board by system, obligation article, assignee, or deadline. Use the **Group by system** toggle to see all obligations for a single AI system on one board view, or switch to **Table view** for a spreadsheet-style list that you can sort and export.

### Org-level vs system-level obligations

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="System-level obligations">
    Most obligations are tied to a specific AI system — for example, the risk management system (Art. 9), technical documentation (Art. 11 / Annex IV), human oversight measures (Art. 14), and post-market monitoring plan (Art. 72). These appear on the Kanban board filtered by system and contribute to that system's compliance percentage.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Org-level obligations">
    Some obligations apply to your whole organisation regardless of how many systems you operate. The primary example is **Article 4 AI Literacy** — ensuring that all staff involved with AI systems have sufficient AI literacy. Org-level obligations appear in a dedicated **Organisation** section at the top of the Obligations board and carry a single completion status for your entire organisation. Completing an org-level obligation contributes to every system's score simultaneously.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## How obligations roll up to the compliance score

Each obligation is weighted by the Obligation Engine based on its article and risk tier. When an obligation reaches **Approved** status, its weight is added to the numerator of the system's compliance score. The score is displayed as a percentage on the system overview page and the main dashboard. Progress through the six compliance phases is gated: you must reach a minimum score in each phase before the next phase unlocks.

<Info>
  Obligations in **Under Review** status contribute a partial score (shown in amber on the dashboard) to give you a realistic progress picture, but they only count in full once approved.
</Info>

## Completing an obligation

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open an obligation">
    Click any obligation card on the Kanban board. The obligation detail panel slides in from the right, showing the article reference, a plain-language description of what is required, the deadline, the current assignee, and any evidence already attached.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your form mode">
    Click **Open form** to launch the smart form. Select your preferred mode:

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="Guided mode (wizard)">
        Guided mode breaks the form into short, focused steps with contextual help text and Aria suggestions on every screen. Choose this mode if you are completing the obligation for the first time or if you want plain-language prompts explaining what each field requires under the Act.
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Expert mode (paginated)">
        Expert mode presents all fields on paginated screens without the step-by-step guidance. Choose this mode if you are an experienced compliance professional who wants to move through the form quickly, or if you are updating a previously completed obligation.
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill the smart form">
    Complete each field in the form. Fields marked with a red asterisk are mandatory for submission. Where Aria has a suggestion — indicated by a purple sparkle icon — click the icon to expand the suggested answer with its article reference. You can accept the suggestion as-is, edit it, or dismiss it.

    <Tip>
      Use the **Aria assistant panel** (open it via the chat icon in the bottom-right corner) to ask questions about what a field requires, request a draft answer based on your system's description, or check whether your phrasing aligns with guidance from the EU AI Office. Aria's responses cite specific articles and are grounded in EU-hosted regulatory data.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Attach evidence">
    Most obligations require supporting evidence — a policy document, test log, training record, or third-party certificate. Click **Attach evidence** within the form to:

    - **Upload a file** directly (PDF, Word, CSV, image).
    - **Connect a cloud source**: Google Drive, SharePoint, GitHub, or Jira. Veritome fetches the document and stores a SHA-256 hash so the evidence is verifiable.
    - **Link an existing evidence item** already in your Evidence Map — one document can satisfy multiple obligations without duplication.

    Set an **expiry date** on time-limited evidence (e.g. an annual audit report). Veritome will alert you when the evidence is approaching expiry so you can renew it before the obligation lapses.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit for review">
    When all required fields are complete and evidence is attached, click **Submit for review**. The obligation moves to **Under Review** on the Kanban board. Veritome notifies the designated reviewer by email and surfaces the item in their review queue.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Four-eyes approval">
    A second team member with reviewer or admin rights opens the obligation from their queue, reads the responses and evidence, and either:

    - Clicks **Approve** — the obligation moves to **Approved** and its weight is added to the compliance score.
    - Clicks **Request changes** — the obligation returns to **In Progress** with a comment explaining what needs to be amended. The assignee is notified.

    <Note>
      The four-eyes rule means the person who completed the form cannot approve their own submission. If you are a solo user or a small team where this creates a bottleneck, contact your account manager about workflow configuration options.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Obligation statuses at a glance

| Status | Description | Score contribution |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| Not Started | Generated but untouched | 0 % |
| In Progress | Form open, not yet submitted | 0 % |
| Under Review | Submitted, awaiting peer approval | Partial (amber) |
| Approved | Peer-reviewed and accepted | Full weight |
| Archived | No longer applicable after re-classification | Not counted |

<Warning>
  Obligations with article-derived deadlines show a countdown timer on their card. If a deadline passes without the obligation reaching Approved status, the card turns red and an alert appears on the dashboard. Address overdue obligations promptly — regulators can request evidence of timely compliance.
</Warning>
