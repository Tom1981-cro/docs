The EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689) imposes binding obligations on any organisation that places, deploys, or substantially modifies an AI system in the European Union. For most European SMEs, translating hundreds of articles into concrete daily actions is genuinely hard work — and getting it wrong carries fines of up to €35 million or 7 % of global annual turnover.

Veritome is a compliance platform built specifically for that challenge. You register your AI systems once, run a guided classification wizard that maps each system to the right risk tier and role, and Veritome's Obligation Engine automatically generates the exact set of duties that apply to you — organised by EU AI Act article, phased across a six-step journey, and tracked with evidence, status, and deadlines. Built and hosted entirely in Europe, Veritome stores your compliance data under GDPR-native conditions from day one.

## Who Veritome is for

Veritome is designed for:

- **AI Providers** — organisations that develop, train, or place an AI system on the EU market under their own name or brand.
- **AI Deployers** — organisations that use a third-party AI system in a professional context (the majority of SMEs).
- **GPAI Model Providers** — organisations that develop and release general-purpose AI models (e.g. foundation models) covered by Arts. 53–55.
- **Compliance leads, legal counsel, and technical teams** at any of the above who need a single structured workspace rather than a spreadsheet.

<Note>
  You do not need to know your role before you start. The classification wizard determines your role, risk tier, and applicable obligations for each system in under five minutes.
</Note>

## The six-phase compliance journey

Veritome structures compliance as a sequential six-phase journey. Each phase unlocks the next and produces concrete outputs — documentation, assessments, and registrations — that together constitute your compliance record.

| Phase | Label | Core output |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | **Classify** | Risk tier, role, Annex III domain, prohibited-practice screen |
| 2 | **Scope & Literacy** | Obligation list confirmed, Art. 4 AI literacy obligations assigned |
| 3 | **Build & Document** | Risk management (Art. 9), data governance (Art. 10), technical documentation (Annex IV), human oversight (Art. 14) |
| 4 | **Conformity Assessment** | Art. 43 conformity assessment (Providers) or Art. 27 FRIA (Deployers), declaration of conformity |
| 5 | **Register** | EU AI database registration before market placement |
| 6 | **Operate & Monitor** | Post-market monitoring (Art. 72), serious incident reporting (Art. 73), record retention |

Read the full phase-by-phase explanation in [The EU AI Act Compliance Journey](/concepts/compliance-journey).

## What Veritome automates

Once you classify a system, Veritome does the heavy lifting:

- **Obligation Engine** — auto-maps every applicable article duty based on your role, risk tier, and Annex III domain. No manual triage required.
- **Smart forms and guided wizard mode** — structured forms with plain-English guidance for every obligation, with Aria AI drafting assistance available on key documents.
- **Evidence library** — attach, version, hash-verify, and link files to specific obligations.
- **Deadline tracking and calendar** — statutory deadlines surfaced as actionable cards, with overdue alerts.
- **Regulatory updates** — live news and horizon-scan signals surface relevant changes so you can act before a deadline moves.
