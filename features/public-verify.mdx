Public Verify gives every AI system registered in Veritome a permanent, publicly accessible identity card hosted at a unique URL. The card displays key system metadata — name, provider, risk tier, classification date, and a cryptographic hash of the compliance dossier — so that regulators, notified bodies, and counterparties can verify your system's compliance posture without needing a Veritome account. Think of it as a digital conformity stamp that you can share anywhere.

## What the ID Card Shows

Each Public Verify page at `/verify/system/[slug]` displays the following information:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="System Name & Provider" icon="building">
    The registered name of the AI system and the legal entity responsible for its compliance (as entered in the system dossier).
  </Card>
  <Card title="Risk Tier & Classification" icon="shield-halved">
    The EU AI Act risk classification (Unacceptable, High-Risk, Limited-Risk, Minimal-Risk) and the date on which that classification was last confirmed.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Dossier Hash" icon="fingerprint">
    A SHA-256 hash of the compiled compliance dossier at the time of last publication. Regulators can recompute this hash against a downloaded audit bundle to confirm no data has been altered.
  </Card>
  <Card title="QR Code" icon="qrcode">
    A scannable QR code linking directly to the Public Verify URL — ready to include in printed documentation, product manuals, or conformity declarations.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Compliance Status" icon="circle-check">
    A summary of overall dossier completion status (e.g. *Dossier complete — last updated 2024-11-15*) expressed as a plain-English label, not a score.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Publication Timestamp" icon="clock">
    The UTC date and time the public record was last published. Records are immutable once published — new publications create a new versioned entry.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Info>
  The Public Verify page is intentionally minimal and does not expose obligation-level detail, internal comments, or team member information. It is designed purely for external verification.
</Info>

## The Hash Seal: Verifying Dossier Integrity

The dossier hash is the cornerstone of the Public Verify feature. When you publish a system's compliance record, Veritome:

1. Compiles all dossier content (obligation responses, attached evidence metadata, risk assessment values) into a canonical JSON structure.
2. Computes a SHA-256 hash of that structure.
3. Displays the hash prominently on the Public Verify page.
4. Seals the record — the published entry is immutable.

If a regulator downloads your audit bundle (see [Reports & Exports](/features/reports-and-exports)), they can recompute the SHA-256 hash of the bundle manifest and compare it against the hash displayed on the Public Verify page. An exact match confirms the dossier has not been altered since publication.

<Note>
  Publishing a new version of your dossier creates a new versioned Public Verify record with a new hash. Previous versions remain accessible with their original hashes, giving regulators a full publication history.
</Note>

## Finding Your System's Verify URL

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the system dossier">
    From the left sidebar, navigate to **Systems** and click the system you want to share.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to the Publish tab">
    Inside the system dossier, click the **Publish** tab at the top of the page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish the record">
    If you have not yet published this system, click **Publish compliance record**. Veritome computes the dossier hash, seals the record, and generates the public URL. This takes a few seconds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy the Verify URL">
    Once published, the full URL (e.g. `https://veritome.eu/verify/system/paymentscore-v2-abc123`) is displayed with a **Copy link** button. The QR code is available for download as a PNG or SVG.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share with regulators">
    Paste the URL into emails, conformity declarations, or procurement responses. Recipients open the page in any browser — no login required.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Sharing the QR Code

The QR code on the Public Verify page links directly to the `/verify/system/[slug]` URL and is generated at publication time. To download it:

1. Open the **Publish** tab of the relevant system dossier.
2. Click **Download QR code** and choose PNG (for digital use) or SVG (for print).
3. Embed the QR code in conformity declarations, user manuals, product packaging, or any physical documentation accompanying the AI system.

<Tip>
  Include the QR code and the full Verify URL side by side in printed documentation. Regulators who prefer not to scan a QR code can type or copy the URL directly.
</Tip>

## Updating the Public Record

The Public Verify page always shows the most recently published version. Veritome does not automatically republish when you update your dossier — you must explicitly trigger a new publication so the hash accurately reflects the current state.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Update your dossier">
    Complete any obligation updates, upload new evidence, and resolve any outstanding attention items.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Return to the Publish tab">
    Navigate to the system's **Publish** tab. You will see a **Changes since last publication** indicator showing how many obligations have been modified.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish a new version">
    Click **Publish new version**. Veritome recomputes the hash and creates a new sealed record. The Public Verify URL remains the same — it now displays the updated record.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Once a version is published and sealed, it cannot be edited or deleted. Ensure your dossier is accurate and complete before publishing. If you discover an error in a published record, publish a corrected version — the previous version remains in the publication history as an auditable record.
</Warning>

## Access and Privacy

The Public Verify page is:

- **Unauthenticated** — no login or Veritome account is required to view it.
- **Read-only** — visitors cannot modify any data.
- **Scope-limited** — only the fields described above are displayed. Internal notes, team comments, and evidence file contents are never exposed.
- **Indexed** — the page may be indexed by search engines unless you enable the **Restrict indexing** option in **Settings → Organisation → Public Verify Settings**.

<Accordion title="Restricting public access to specific systems">
  If you need to prevent a system's Public Verify page from being publicly accessible (for example, during an active investigation or for systems still in draft), go to the system's **Publish** tab and click **Unpublish**. The URL will return a 404 until you republish. Existing published records are retained internally for your audit history.
</Accordion>
