<Accordion title="Does the EU AI Act apply to my company?">
  Almost certainly yes, if you develop, sell, or use AI systems in a professional context in the EU.

  **If you are based in the EU:** The Act applies to you as a provider (if you develop AI), a deployer (if you use AI in your business), or both.

  **If you are based outside the EU:** You are still in scope if your AI system produces outputs that are used in the EU — for example, if EU customers use your product or your employees in an EU office use an AI tool. The Act has explicit extra-territorial reach.

  **SME carve-outs:** The Act acknowledges the challenges for SMEs through proportionate procedural accommodations (for example, simplified conformity assessment routes and regulatory sandboxes), but it does not exempt SMEs from compliance obligations. Size does not change whether the Act applies — it may affect how some obligations are fulfilled.

  If you are unsure, the fastest way to find out is to take the [free readiness assessment](https://veritome.eu/apps/readiness-assessment) — it takes under ten minutes and requires no account.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What is the difference between a provider and a deployer?">
  These are the two most important roles in the EU AI Act, and many SMEs are both at the same time.

  **Provider:** An organisation or individual that develops an AI system and places it on the EU market, or puts it into service in the EU. If you build an AI product — even if you use third-party models underneath — you are a provider of that system.

  **Deployer:** An organisation or individual that uses an AI system under their own authority as part of a professional activity. If you use an AI-powered tool in your business — a vendor's HR screening platform, a third-party credit scoring API, a chatbot you have configured and deployed — you are a deployer.

  **Being both:** Many companies build AI for external customers (provider role) while also using AI tools internally (deployer role). Veritome allows you to register systems in either or both roles, and generates the appropriate obligation set for each.

  The practical difference: providers carry the heaviest technical obligations (risk management, documentation, conformity assessment). Deployers have a distinct but significant set of operational obligations, primarily under Article 26, including human oversight, log retention, worker notification, and incident reporting.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I use the Free plan for real compliance work?">
  Yes — the Free plan is a genuine working tier, not just a demo. It lets you register one AI system and add one team member, with no credit card required. You get access to the classification wizard, obligation tracking, and the compliance dossier for that one system.

  Where the Free plan has limits: it supports only one system and one member, which means it is suited to individual practitioners or very early-stage compliance exploration. If you need to manage multiple AI systems, collaborate with a team, or generate exportable compliance documentation at scale, one of the paid plans will be a better fit.

  You can upgrade at any time from inside the platform — your existing classification and dossier data carries over automatically.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens when I upgrade my plan?">
  Upgrading is immediate. As soon as your payment is confirmed, the higher-tier limits and features are unlocked on your account — you do not need to re-import data or restart your work.

  Your existing AI system registrations, classification results, obligation tasks, and evidence uploads are all preserved. Upgrading simply unlocks additional system slots, team member seats, and any features that are exclusive to the higher tier (such as API access, custom evidence connectors, and priority support response times).

  If you are upgrading from an annual plan and are mid-cycle, Veritome calculates a prorated credit for the remainder of your current billing period. Contact [support](https://veritome.eu/contact) if you have questions about a specific upgrade scenario.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do I invite team members?">
  Go to **Settings → Team** in the Veritome dashboard. Enter the email address of the person you want to invite and select their role. Roles control what they can view and edit within your organisation's systems and dossiers.

  The invitee receives an email with a one-click link to create their account or, if they already have a Veritome account, to join your organisation. Invitations expire after 72 hours — if they miss it, simply resend from the same Team settings screen.

  The number of seats available depends on your plan. If you have reached your seat limit and need to add more members, you can upgrade your plan or contact us to discuss your needs.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What documents does Veritome generate?">
  Veritome generates the core compliance documentation required by the EU AI Act, structured and pre-populated from your classification and task responses:

  - **Technical documentation** — the Annex IV documentation package required for high-risk AI systems
  - **Risk register** — the Article 9 risk management record, including identified risks, mitigations, and residual risk notes
  - **EU Declaration of Conformity** — the Article 47 written declaration, ready for signing
  - **Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (FRIA)** — the Article 27 assessment template, pre-structured to the regulatory checklist
  - **Instructions for use (IFU)** — provider-side documentation for deployers, covering Article 13 requirements
  - **Compliance dossier** — the complete exportable record of your classification, obligations, evidence, and decisions

  All documents are generated in your organisation's chosen language and are version-controlled so you can track changes over time. Documents can be exported in PDF and JSON formats.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How does the four-eyes approval workflow work?">
  The four-eyes (dual-approval) workflow ensures that key compliance decisions — such as finalising a system classification, approving a risk assessment, or signing off a dossier for export — require sign-off from two different team members before they are committed.

  **How it works in practice:**
  1. The first team member completes the relevant step and submits it for approval.
  2. The platform notifies the designated approver (or any member with the appropriate role).
  3. The approver reviews the submission and either approves or requests changes.
  4. Only after both steps are complete does the record become finalised and the next step unlock.

  This workflow is particularly important for high-risk AI systems, where the Act's audit trail requirements mean you need documented evidence that decisions were reviewed by more than one responsible person. The four-eyes trail is automatically included in your dossier export.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I export all my compliance data?">
  Yes. You can export your entire compliance dossier — including classification records, obligation task status, evidence uploads, generated documents, and the four-eyes approval audit trail — at any time from the **Dossier → Export** screen.

  Exports are available in PDF (for human-readable documentation packages) and JSON (for machine-readable records you can import into other systems or archive). There is no export limit on any paid plan.

  If you decide to leave Veritome, you own your data and can export everything before closing your account. We do not lock compliance records to the platform.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What is the public verify URL used for?">
  Every compliance dossier in Veritome can be given a public verify URL — a shareable link that lets third parties (customers, regulators, partners, auditors) verify that your AI system has been assessed for EU AI Act compliance and review a summary of the classification outcome and key obligations.

  The verify page does not expose your internal documentation or evidence — it shows only the information you choose to make public: the system name, its risk classification, the registration date, and the status of the key obligations.

  This feature is particularly useful for providers who want to demonstrate compliance to enterprise customers as part of procurement due diligence, or for deployers who need to show regulators that they have carried out the required assessments.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How accurate is the classification wizard?">
  The classification wizard is built directly on the text of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689, the Annex III domain definitions, and the Article 6 classification rules. It is the same logic that Veritome's regulatory team uses for client assessments, and it is updated whenever the regulatory registry changes (including for Digital Omnibus developments).

  That said, AI Act classification involves legal interpretation, and some edge cases are genuinely ambiguous. The wizard is designed to surface those ambiguities and flag them for review, rather than give you false certainty.

  **The most important caveat:** the wizard's output is a compliance tool, not a legal opinion. For systems near a classification boundary — particularly those where you are considering an Article 6(3) exception — you should involve qualified legal counsel to validate the reasoning before relying on it in regulatory proceedings.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Does Veritome constitute legal advice?">
  No. Veritome is a compliance management platform — it helps you understand regulatory obligations, organise your documentation, and build a defensible compliance programme. It does not provide legal advice, and nothing in the platform should be treated as a legal opinion.

  For questions about how the EU AI Act applies to your specific situation, the correct interpretation of an article, or whether your system qualifies for an exception, you should consult a qualified EU law practitioner. Veritome can make that conversation much more productive by giving your legal team structured, accurate documentation to work from — but it does not replace professional legal advice.
</Accordion>
